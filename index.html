Timeline of Lionel Messi, one of the world's greatest football players.

1987 - 2001

Lionel Andres Messi Cuccittini was born on Wednesday, June 24, 1987. He was born in Rosario, Argentina.


At the age of six, Messi became a member of the Old Boys. He played for this team for about six years. While he was at the youth club, he scored over 500 goals. The team was almost unbeatable.


Messi and his family relocated to the city of Barcelona in Spain in February 2001. The family moved into an apartment that was very close to Barcelona’s football team’s stadium, Camp Nou.

2002 - 2005

Messi became a student at Barcelona’s youth academy, La Masia. In February 2002, he was officially enrolled in the Royal Spanish Football Federation (RFEF).


The footballer made his first appearance in a match against FC Porto.


On his 18th birthday, Messi landed his first contract as a senior team player. The contract was completed on June 24, 2005. The contract outlined that he would be an official player with the Barcelona football club until 2010. His buyout clause was set at a whopping €150 million.

2006 - 2009

Lionel Messi achieves his first champions league on May 17, 2006.


Messi had the privilege of playing at the Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics which was held in Beijing and a number of other cities in the People’s Republic of China. With Messi as a key player, Argentina won the tournament.


The final match of the 2009 UEFA Champions League was played on May 27, 2009. The match was held in Rome, Italy at the Stadio Olimpico. The winner of this match would be crowned the winners of the 2008-09 season of the UEFA Champions League. The winner of the match was Barcelona. They defeated Manchester United 2-0. Lionel Messi scored the final goal of the match. The UEFA Champions is a tournament that involves the top football clubs in all of Europe.

2009 - 2011

On December 1, 2009, Lionel Messi was awarded his very first Ballon d’Or.


Messi received his second consecutive Ballon d’Or on January 10, 2011. It was a great year for La Masia, the FC Barcelona academy. It became the first youth academy to have all three of the finalists for the Ballon d’Or in a single year. The players were Lionel Messi, Andres Iniesta, and Xavi.


The final match of the 2011 UEFA Champions League was held on May 28, 2011. The match was played at the Wembley Stadium in London. The match was to reveal who would be crowned the winner of the 2010-11 season of the UEFA Champions League. This was the 56th season of the tournament. It was also the 19th season in the Champions League era. The two teams that went head to head in this final match were Barcelona and Manchester United. Barcelona came out victorious and was dubbed the winners of the 2011 UEFA Champions League.

2012 - 2014

The award ceremony was held in Zurich on January 9, 2012. Lionel Messi received the title of world player of the year at the award ceremony. This marked the third consecutive year that Lionel Messi was named the winner of the Ballon d’Or.


Messi landed his fourth Ballon d’Or on January 7, 2013. The footballer was, for the fourth consecutive year, named the world’s best football player.


Argentina was defeated by Germany in the 2014 World Cup finals. Germany clinched a 1-0 victory.

2015 - 2016

On June 6, 2015, Lionel Messi secured his fourth Champions League.


The sizzling match ran into extra time. The match was decided by a penalty shootout. Chile defeated Argentina 4-1 on penalties.


Messi’s skills as a footballer have not been going unnoticed. For the fifth time, Messi received the Ballon d’Or award.

2019 - 2022

Messi attended the 64th annual ceremony of the Ballon d’Or. It was presented by France Football. Lionel Messi was recognized as the best player in the world for 2019. This marked the sixth time that Messi has received this award in his career.


Lionel Messi was declared the Most Marketable Athlete of 2020. His rival, Cristiano Ronaldo finished at the second spot on the list. The two are on the list ahead of other major athletes like Lebron James and Virat Kohli.

    The Greatest Rival

    The rivalry between Lionel Messi and Cristiano Ronaldo stands as an epic saga in the annals of sports history, transcending the boundaries of football to become a global spectacle. For over a decade, these two titans have mesmerized the world with their unparalleled skill, relentless goal-scoring prowess, and insatiable thirst for victory. The Messi-Ronaldo rivalry is not just a clash of footballing legends; it is a narrative that has defined an era, captivating fans and pundits alike. Whether competing for individual awards or leading their respective teams to glory on the grandest stages, Messi and Ronaldo have created a rivalry that goes beyond the pitch, sparking endless debates about who truly is the greatest. Each match featuring these two icons becomes a spectacle, a duel that elevates the beautiful game to a level of intensity rarely witnessed. As they continue to etch their names in the record books, Messi and Ronaldo have crafted a legacy that will be recounted for generations, solidifying their status as the greatest rivalry in the history of sports.
